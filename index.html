What matters most in the governor’s budget

A reader-friendly guide to the biggest numbers, policy shifts, and political fault lines in the Sherrill administration’s first budget proposal, built from the state’s Budget in Brief.

Top newsroom angles
  • Nearly $2 billion in spending cuts paired with targeted new investments.
  • Property-tax relief remains massive, but Stay NJ is tightened for wealthier seniors.
  • Record school funding and pre-K money sit alongside a new school-based mental health push.
  • Health-care costs, Medicaid pressure, and federal policy changes are recurring risk points.
  • More than $1 billion in state support is proposed for NJ Transit.
