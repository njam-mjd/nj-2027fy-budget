Top Stories & Policy Shifts

Reform to Stay NJ Program

Governor Sherrill is proposing strategic reductions to the Stay NJ program to ensure it is "fairer and more efficient." The plan introduces a **$250,000 income threshold** and a **$4,000 maximum benefit**, shifting resources toward lower-income seniors [p.10].

Youth Safety & Mental Health

The budget funds a new **Office of Youth Online Mental Health Safety** and a **Social Media Research Center** ($500k) to combat the "addictive influence" of social media. It also prepares districts for the first **cell-phone free school year** [p.11].

Closing Tax Loopholes

To fund these initiatives, the state will close corporate loopholes and implement a temporary cap on **Net Operating Loss (NOL)** deductions, expected to raise **$485 million** from the state's largest corporate filers [p.47].
